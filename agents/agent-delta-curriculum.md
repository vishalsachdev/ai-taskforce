## Key Findings

### CS2023: The New AI Curriculum Standard

**What**: CS2023 curriculum guidelines (December 2023)
**Significance**: **First-ever collaboration** between ACM, IEEE-CS, and AAAI
**Rationale**: AI's transformative impact requires integrated treatment, not addendum

**Key Changes from Prior Standards**:
- AI elevated to equal status with traditional CS areas
- Ethics integrated throughout (not standalone course)
- Emphasis on responsible AI development
- Recognition of AI's cross-disciplinary nature

**Implication for UIUC**: Recommendations should align with CS2023 for accreditation and professional recognition.

### Three-Tier Learning Outcomes Framework

#### Tier 1: Foundational (ALL Students - AI Literacy)

**Technical Understanding**:
- Explain what AI is and what it isn't
- Recognize AI systems in daily life
- Understand basic concepts (data, algorithms, models, training)

**Evaluative Skills**:
- Assess AI system outputs critically
- Identify potential biases and limitations
- Evaluate appropriate vs. inappropriate AI use cases

**Practical Application**:
- Use AI tools effectively (ChatGPT, image generation, etc.)
- Interpret AI results with appropriate skepticism
- Apply AI to solve simple problems

**Ethical Awareness**:
- Recognize ethical implications of AI systems
- Understand bias, fairness, privacy concerns
- Consider societal and environmental impacts

**Recommendation**: 3-6 credit general education course (AI Literacy for All)

#### Tier 2: Intermediate (AI Minor Students - Specialized Competency)

**Technical Mastery**:
- Implement supervised and unsupervised learning algorithms
- Design and train neural networks
- Evaluate model performance using appropriate metrics
- Debug and optimize AI systems

**Conceptual Depth**:
- Explain theoretical foundations (probability, optimization, information theory)
- Compare different AI approaches for given problems
- Understand computational complexity and scalability

**Applied Problem-Solving**:
- Develop AI solutions for real-world problems
- Work with messy, real data (not just clean datasets)
- Deploy models in production environments
- Collaborate in interdisciplinary teams

**Professional Skills**:
- Communicate AI concepts to non-technical audiences
- Document AI systems and decisions
- Assess ethical implications proactively
- Stay current with rapidly evolving field

**Recommendation**: 18-24 credit minor with core + electives + capstone

#### Tier 3: Advanced (X+AI Degree Students - Domain Integration)

**Domain Expertise**:
- **Business+AI**: Apply AI to marketing, finance, operations, supply chain
- **Psychology+AI**: Computational models of cognition, behavior prediction
- **Biology+AI**: Bioinformatics, drug discovery, protein folding
- **Journalism+AI**: Data journalism, fact-checking, content analysis

**Integration Skills**:
- Translate domain problems into AI-solvable formulations
- Evaluate AI solutions using domain-specific metrics
- Communicate AI insights to domain stakeholders
- Recognize domain-specific ethical considerations

**Innovation Capacity**:
- Identify novel AI applications in domain
- Propose AI-enhanced solutions to domain challenges
- Contribute to AI+X interdisciplinary research

**Recommendation**: 30-credit AI core + 50+ credits in domain (X+AI degree)

### Four Prerequisite Pathway Models

#### Model 1: Minimal Prerequisites (Columbia University approach)
**Requirements**: High school algebra + introductory programming
**Target**: Broadest accessibility
**Trade-off**: More foundational content in core courses
**Best For**: General AI literacy, AI minor applications track

#### Model 2: Low-Code/No-Code (Sacred Heart University $250K AI lab)
**Requirements**: No programming required
**Tools**: Visual programming, AutoML platforms, cloud AI services
**Target**: Non-technical students, business applications
**Best For**: AI certificate, business+AI programs

#### Model 3: Mathematics-First (Traditional CS approach)
**Requirements**: Calculus I-II, Linear Algebra, Probability/Statistics
**Target**: STEM students, technical depth
**Trade-off**: Excludes non-STEM students without bridge courses
**Best For**: AI minor technical track, engineering+AI

#### Model 4: Flexible Entry (UW Certificate program)
**Requirements**: Varies by course; prerequisites checked per course not program
**Target**: Professional students, diverse backgrounds
**Pathway**: Bridge courses available to fill gaps
**Best For**: Certificate programs, lifelong learners

**Recommendation for UIUC**: Dual pathway (Model 3 for technical track, Model 2 for applications track)

### Pedagogical Best Practices

#### Project-Based Learning (Evidence: 37% Improvement)

**Research Finding**: Project-based learning shows 37% increase in interdisciplinary learning outcomes compared to lecture-only instruction.

**Effective AI Projects**:
- Build and train models on real datasets
- Deploy AI applications to production
- Conduct AI system audits for bias/fairness
- Develop AI solutions for community partners

**Implementation**:
- 30-40% of course time dedicated to project work
- Teams of 3-4 students (interdisciplinary when possible)
- Authentic problems with real stakeholders
- Iterative development with multiple check-ins

#### Ethics Integration (Not Standalone)

**Research Finding**: Embedded ethics more effective than single standalone course.

**Stakeholder-Based Approach** (5 themes):
1. **Human Values**: How AI affects dignity, autonomy, justice
2. **Power Structures**: Who benefits? Who is harmed? Who decides?
3. **Environmental Impact**: Carbon footprint, resource consumption
4. **Economic Effects**: Labor displacement, wealth concentration
5. **Global Perspectives**: Cultural differences, digital divide

**Implementation**:
- Ethics modules in EVERY course (15-20% of content)
- Case studies of AI failures and harms
- Stakeholder analysis exercises
- Ethical impact statements for projects

#### Tools & Platforms for Hands-On Learning

**Programming**:
- Python (NumPy, pandas, scikit-learn, PyTorch, TensorFlow)
- Jupyter Notebooks for interactive learning
- Version control (Git/GitHub)

**Cloud Platforms**:
- Google Colab (free GPU access)
- AWS Educate, Azure for Students, Google Cloud Education
- Kaggle for datasets and competitions

**Specialized Tools**:
- Fairness toolkits (IBM AI Fairness 360, Google What-If Tool)
- Explainability tools (LIME, SHAP)
- AutoML platforms (Google AutoML, H2O.ai)

**Recommendation**: Provide infrastructure via cloud credits + campus computing resources.

### Scalable Delivery Models

#### MAIC: Massive AI-empowered Courses (Evolution of MOOCs)

**Concept**: AI-powered education at scale
**Technologies**:
- Intelligent tutoring systems
- Automated code review and feedback
- Personalized learning paths
- Discussion forum analytics

**Challenges**:
- Low completion rates (5-10% typical)
- Limited personal interaction
- Assessment integrity concerns

**Best Practice**: Blended model (online content + in-person active learning)

#### Hybrid Excellence Model

**Structure**:
- **Asynchronous Online**: Lectures, readings, individual exercises (30-40%)
- **Synchronous Online**: Live Q&A, discussions, group work (20-30%)
- **In-Person**: Labs, projects, exams, community building (30-40%)

**Benefits**:
- Scales without sacrificing quality
- Accommodates diverse student schedules
- Leverages best of each modality

**Infrastructure Requirements**:
- Robust LMS (Canvas, Moodle) with video integration
- Virtual collaboration tools (Zoom, Gather, Discord)
- Cloud computing access for all students
- TA support scaled appropriately (1 TA per 30-40 students)

### Accessibility & Inclusion Strategies

#### Universal Design for Learning (UDL) Principles

**Multiple Means of Representation**:
- Visual + auditory + text formats for all content
- Captions/transcripts for all video
- Adjustable text size and contrast

**Multiple Means of Action & Expression**:
- Choice in project topics and formats
- Varied assessment types (exams, projects, presentations)
- Assistive technology support

**Multiple Means of Engagement**:
- Real-world, relevant problems
- Student choice and autonomy
- Collaborative and individual options

#### Addressing Equity Barriers

**Language Barriers**:
- Plain language technical explanations
- Multilingual resources where possible
- Writing center partnerships

**Socioeconomic Barriers**:
- Free cloud computing credits (no student-paid licenses)
- Laptop loaner programs
- Recorded lectures for students with work conflicts

**Prior Knowledge Gaps**:
- Bridge courses for math and programming
- Peer mentoring programs
- Office hours structured for different levels

**Cultural Barriers**:
- Diverse examples and case studies
- International perspectives on AI
- Explicit instruction on academic norms

### Assessment Approaches

#### Competency-Based Assessment

**Knowledge** (Can they explain it?):
- Concept quizzes (low-stakes, frequent)
- Written explanations of algorithms
- Peer teaching exercises

**Skills** (Can they do it?):
- Coding assignments (auto-graded + human review)
- Model building and tuning projects
- Debugging challenges

**Application** (Can they use it wisely?):
- Real-world case studies
- Stakeholder presentations
- Ethical impact assessments

#### AI-Enabled Assessment Tools

**Automated Code Review**:
- Style, efficiency, correctness checking
- Immediate feedback for students
- Scales to large enrollments

**Plagiarism Detection**:
- Code similarity analysis
- Conceptual understanding oral exams
- Process documentation requirements (Git commits, design docs)

**Personalized Feedback**:
- AI tutors for common misconceptions
- Adaptive problem sets
- Learning analytics for early intervention
